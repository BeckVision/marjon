What RD-001 is doing

Plain-English Definition

The raw transaction layer for U-001

For each coin, Marjon looks at the Pumpswap pool, collects trade signatures, filters them to the coin's observation window, parses them into canonical buy and sell rows, and stores them as a reference table. In practical terms: it reconstructs the tape for the coin's early life.

  • › Individual trades — no candle summarization. Wallet-level participation is preserved.
  • › Budget guards prevent oversized free-tier windows from blocking the normal retry queue.
  • › Ordinary backlog keeps shrinking while expensive edge cases stay isolated and visible.

Operational Behavior

How it behaves when working properly

Source

Recent windows use Shyft. Older windows use Helius. The system chooses based on coin age.

Unit

Each RD-001 row is one parsed buy or sell with wallet, size, fee, and timing information.

Retry

Normal historical partial rows keep getting retried and converted into full window coverage.

Guard

Oversized windows are separated out when they exceed the free-tier budget, instead of poisoning the normal retry queue.

Goal

Turn "the coin pumped" into a measurable explanation of how traders behaved during the move.

Research Use

What RD-001 can actually tell you

If you are researching early-token behavior, candles alone are weak evidence. RD-001 gives you the trade-flow layer that supports real signals, filters, and explanations.

Participation

Who showed up?

Count unique wallets, repeated wallets, bursts of new participants, and whether a move came from broad activity or a tiny cluster.

Pressure

What side was stronger?

Measure buy vs. sell imbalance, aggressive selling, exhaustion, and whether price gains were supported by real net demand.

Size

How large were the trades?

Separate noisy micro-churn from meaningful size. Detect whether a coin was moved by whales, retail swarms, or repeated medium clips.

Why not just use OHLCV?

Because OHLCV tells you the shape of the move, not the microstructure underneath it. RD-001 is what turns a price move into behavior you can test.

Why are some rows marked partial?

Partial means some data exists but the full observation window is not yet trusted as complete. That is an operational coverage state, not useless data.

Why isolate oversized rows?

On free-tier APIs, a few very busy coins can consume disproportionate budget. The isolated lane keeps normal coverage moving instead of stalling everything.
